# Story Structures

Choose one structure that best matches the brief.

| Structure | Use It When | Core Logic |
|---|---|---|
| CONTRAST | Before and after are clearly different | Problem → product → visible improvement |
| EXAGGERATION | Sensory or emotional reaction should feel amplified | Small trigger → oversized response |
| TWIST | The solution should feel surprising or clever | Set expectation → reveal product as the twist |
| CHAOS | The starting situation is stressful, noisy, messy, or inefficient | Disorder → product intervention → control |
| FANTASY | The brand should feel idealized, dreamy, premium, or escapist | Real world → product use → stylized alternate world |

## Selection heuristics

- Beverage, beauty, and performance products often work best with CONTRAST.
- Food and social products often work well with EXAGGERATION.
- Productivity apps, AI tools, and utilities often suit CHAOS or TWIST.
- Luxury, fragrance, travel, and premium beauty often suit FANTASY.

## Core flow

Always preserve this flow even after choosing a structure:
- problem
- trigger
- transformation
- payoff
- ending
