# Storyboard Panel Template

Always design exactly 10 panels.

| Panel | Story Beat | Recommended Shot Type | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Problem situation | Wide shot | Establish the scene and pain point context |
| 2 | Pain point detail | Extreme close-up | Show physical or emotional detail |
| 3 | POV confusion or frustration | POV shot | Let the viewer feel the user's state |
| 4 | Product discovery | Low angle or over-shoulder | Introduce the product as a trigger |
| 5 | Product interaction | Macro close-up | Show hand-to-product or object-to-product contact |
| 6 | Usage moment | Slow-motion close-up | Show the key moment of use |
| 7 | Transformation explosion | Dynamic angle | Visualize the change dramatically |
| 8 | Environment change | Wide environmental shot | Show that the surrounding world also shifts |
| 9 | Character reaction | Medium close-up | Show confidence, relief, joy, or focus |
| 10 | Confident payoff | Hero action shot | Deliver the final payoff and ending |

## Panel-writing rules

For each panel include:
- panel number
- story beat
- shot type
- visual description
- product role
- short caption in the selected text language

## Anti-repetition rules

- Avoid repeating the same framing across adjacent panels.
- Avoid making the product invisible in the middle sequence.
- Ensure panel 7 is visually stronger than panel 6.
- Ensure panel 10 feels like a payoff, not a repeat of the key visual.
